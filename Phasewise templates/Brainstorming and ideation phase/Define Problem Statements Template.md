---

## Overview

A well-articulated customer problem statement allows the team to find the ideal solution for the challenges customers face. It also enables empathy with customers, helping to better understand how they perceive the product or service.

---

## Customer Problem Statement Framework

| Element | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **I am** | Describe the customer with 3–4 key characteristics — who are they? |
| **I'm trying to** | List their outcome or 'job' they care about — what are they trying to achieve? |
| **But** | Describe what problems or barriers stand in the way — what bothers them most? |
| **Because** | Enter the 'root cause' of why the problem or barriers exist — what needs to be solved? |
| **Which makes me feel** | Describe the emotions from the customer's point of view — how does it impact them emotionally? |

---

## Problem Statements

| Problem Statement (PS) | I am (Customer) | I'm trying to | But | Because | Which makes me feel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PS-1** | A busy working professional (25–45 yrs) who is tech-savvy but time-constrained | Book a consultation with a specialist doctor without taking time off work | It takes weeks to get a physical appointment; I have to call multiple clinics and navigate confusing phone queues | There is no centralized platform to discover, compare, and instantly book verified doctors near me | Frustrated, anxious, and helpless — especially during urgent health concerns |
| **PS-2** | A patient living in a Tier-2 or Tier-3 city with limited specialist access | Consult a specialist without traveling 100+ km to the nearest city | Specialist doctors are unavailable locally and telemedicine platforms are not trusted or comprehensive | Existing platforms either lack specialist diversity, have no credibility verification, or have a poor user experience | Neglected and underserved — forced to delay critical healthcare decisions |
| **PS-3** | A general practitioner or specialist doctor (30–55 yrs) | Manage my appointments and availability digitally | I still rely on phone calls, WhatsApp, and manual registers to track patient appointments | There is no dedicated, professional digital tool that integrates my schedule, patient communication, and document exchange | Overwhelmed and disorganized — losing patients to competitors with better digital presence |
| **PS-4** | A healthcare system administrator or clinic manager | Verify doctor credentials and manage patient-doctor interactions from a single dashboard | I cannot monitor appointment flows, identify fraudulent listings, or enforce compliance from a single place | Current tools are fragmented — one system for scheduling, another for communication, none for doctor vetting | Exposed to compliance and reputational risks with no clear oversight |
